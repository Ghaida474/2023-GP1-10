Why were Business Units (BUs) established in KSU?

Aligned with Saudi Arabia's Vision 2030, King Saud University established Business Units (BUs) to diversify the economy, reduce oil dependence, and enhance public services. The BUs utilize the university's resources to promote sustainable development, contribute to the knowledge economy, and generate self-revenue

01
What are the functions and activities of Business Units (BUs) in KSU?

The Business Units (BUs) provide services like training, advisory offerings, projects, research and studies, and consultancy to society. These BUs aim to drive sustainable development through multidirectional knowledge transfer between KSU faculty, students, and private/public partners.

02
King Abdullah Institution for Research and Consulting Studies role?

The King Abdullah Institution for Research and Consulting Studies (KAI) connects King Saud University's (KSU) academic knowledge with public and private partners through project collaborations, consulting, and contractual agreements. This facilitates applying academic insights more broadly.

03

More about Business Units services

Business Units in various colleges provides:

  • Traning Programs:
    • Provides training courses and workshops in latest fields and topics relevant to the college's specialization. Courses can be customized as per beneficiary needs.
    • Training courses and workshops are offered during flexible periods (morning or evening) according to the requirements of the beneficiary.
    • Provide summer programs in various fields, llowing participants to benefit from the college's resources and educational environment during the summer vacation.
  • Consultation Services:
    • Provides consulting and training services to private and public sector entities through partnerships.
    • Builds new partnerships to ensure continued delivery of advisory services.
  • Projects and Domain-Specific Solutions:
    • Implements project teams from faculty to deliver solutions tailored to beneficiary needs.
    • Submits proposals and bids for projects requested by public/private sector.
    • Formulates and reviews proposals for beneficiaries.
  • Seminars:
    • Organizes academic and professional seminars.
  • Research and Studies:
    • Supports establishing and improving research activities relevant to beneficiaries.
    • Collaborates on joint research projects with industry and government.
  • Executive Education Programs:
    • Offers professional diplomas and certificate programs tailored to specific sectors.
  • Resource Utilization:
    • Rents out college facilities during vacations to external entities.
  • In summary, the Business Units leverage the college's facilities, faculty and capabilities to offer customized services in training, research, consultancy and other domains to public and private sector organizations based on their needs.
